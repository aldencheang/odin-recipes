Smoked Bricket

Description

I was watching YouTube one day and came accross a video of people doing a food tour in Texas to find the best BBQ the state has to offer.
Watching those videos and drooling to them inspired me to attempt to make my own brisket. BBQ takes a lot of effort and can get very costly
if you buy them from restaurants, so I decided to try to make it on my own. I quickly hopped back on YouTube to gather some insights on how
to perfectly smoke a brisket, but honestly a lot of the videos online have conflicting instructions. So I turned off all the videos and decided
just to figure it out on my own. Now this recipe took me a good while to perfect, but the meat never went to waste. I hope to expire those who
are looking to smoke their first brisket and provide them a guide showing them how I smoke my brisket and make it taste just like Texas.

Ingredients

  • Cracked black pepper (freshly cracked if possible)
  • Seasoning salt
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • USDA Prime Brisket (19lbs)
  • Applewood smoking pellets

Steps

  1. Preheat your smoker to 225°F.
  2. Using a sharp trimming knife, trim the excess fat from the brisket. Leave about 1/4 inch of fat on the top to help keep the meat moist during the cook.
  3. Once the brisket is trimmed, lightly spritz it with water, apple cider vinegar, or your preferred binder to help the seasoning stick.
  4. Season the brisket generously with black pepper, making sure to cover all sides evenly.
  5. Next, season generously with seasoning salt, filling in the gaps between the black pepper for an even coating.
  6. Once the brisket is fully seasoned, place it in the smoker after the smoker has reached 225°F.
  7. Smoke the brisket until the internal temperature reaches 180°F. This can take anywhere from 8 to 14 hours, so check the temperature periodically.
  8. Once the brisket reaches about 180°F internally, remove it from the smoker and wrap it tightly in butcher paper or aluminum foil. This helps retain moisture and pushes the brisket through the final stage of cooking.
  9. Place the wrapped brisket back into the smoker and continue cooking until the internal temperature reaches 195–205°F, or until a probe slides into the meat with little resistance.
  10. Once the brisket is tender, remove it from the smoker and let it rest, still wrapped, for at least 1 hour before slicing (the longer the rest the better).
