The Perfect Sous-Vide Steak

Description

I first got interested in making sous vide steak because I kept seeing videos online of people cooking perfectly even steaks from edge to edge.
I always enjoyed a good steak, but making one at home can sometimes feel like a gamble because it is easy to overcook it or not get the inside exactly how you want it.
After watching a few videos, I realized that sous vide could make the cooking process a lot more consistent and less stressful.
So I decided to try it out for myself and see if I could make a restaurant-quality steak at home without having to spend restaurant prices.
It took me a few tries to figure out the right temperature, timing, and sear, but once I got it down, it quickly became one of my favorite ways to cook steak.
I hope this recipe helps anyone who wants to make their first sous vide steak and gives them a simple guide to getting a tender, juicy steak with a perfect crust.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. New York strip steaks
  • ½ teaspoon coarse sea salt
  • ½ teaspoon coarse black pepper
  • 2 herb sprigs, rosemary, thyme, or oregano
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil

Steps

  1. Fill a large pot with water to create your sous vide water bath. Make sure the pot is big enough to fully hold your steak, and that the water is deep enough to reach the minimum water line on your sous vide.
  2. Place your sous vide into the water bath and set the temperature to 125–128°F for a medium-rare steak.
  3. While the water is heating up, grab your steak and season both sides generously with salt and black pepper.
  4. Place each steak into its own Stasher bag, FoodSaver bag, or another sous vide-safe bag. If you're cooking multiple steaks, make sure each steak has its own bag.
  5. Add 1 tablespoon of butter on top of each steak, along with a sprig of your favorite herb like rosemary or thyme.
  6. Remove as much air as possible from each bag and seal it tightly. If you're using a Stasher bag or plastic bag, slowly lower it into the water to help push the air out before sealing.
  7. Once the water bath reaches 125–128°F, place the sealed steak bags into the water and let them cook for 2–4 hours. Around 2 hours is enough for the steak to reach temperature, but you can leave it in for up to 4 hours.
  8. After the steak is done cooking, remove it from the water bath and transfer it to a plate. Take the steak out of the bag and pat it dry with a paper towel so it can get a better sear.
  9. Heat a cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat. Once the pan is hot, add your olive oil.
  10. When the oil is hot, place the steak into the pan and sear it for about 1 minute per side, then remove it from the heat.
  11. Serve your steak with chimichurri, roasted vegetables, or your favorite side.
